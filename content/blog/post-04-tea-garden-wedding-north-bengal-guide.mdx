A tea-garden wedding photographs like a film. It lives like a logistics problem. We say this with love.

We design four to six of these every season across Darjeeling, Kurseong and the Dooars belt, and the ones that go well start with a family that took the logistics seriously. This is the brief we wish every couple read before they booked the estate.

## Why a tea-garden wedding looks the way it does (and what your photos won't show)

The reason these weddings look the way they do is that the landscape is doing 80% of the visual work. A tea garden is rows of waist-high bushes, soft hills, a tin-roofed bungalow, occasionally a cloud line at eye level. A camera adores it.

What the photographs won't show:

- The 90-minute drive from the nearest hotel to the bungalow
- The dew on every chair until 10:30 AM
- The cellular dead zone that means your photographer cannot WhatsApp the family group
- The single-cylinder generator that powers the AV
- The cook who has driven up from Siliguri at 4 AM with the catering

These are the things our setup teams build the wedding around. They are also the things you cannot Instagram.

## Which estates host weddings — Darjeeling, Dooars, Kurseong

The list of tea estates that actually host weddings is shorter than the social-media impression suggests. The ones that do, in our experience:

- A small group of Darjeeling-cluster estates with restored bungalows on 1–2 acres of garden, hosting 60–120 guests.
- Two or three Dooars-region estates with larger covered pavilions, hosting 150–250 guests.
- A handful of Kurseong properties suited for an intimate 40–80 guest format.

The constraint is rarely "do they want to host you" — it is "do they have a covered area large enough for your guest count if it rains."

## Logistics: roads, rain, guest transport, the 4-hour rule

The roads are the silent variable. Bagdogra airport → Darjeeling estate is 3.5 to 5 hours depending on season. Bagdogra → Dooars estate is 2.5 to 4 hours.

Our rule of thumb: **if your guest list includes more than 10 people over 65, the venue must be within 4 hours of Bagdogra**. Above that, the journey itself becomes the wedding's most-complained-about feature.

Guest transport is your problem, not the venue's. Plan for two routes, two times, two backup vehicles. The estates we work with can recommend operators we have actually used.

Rain: in the Dooars belt, monsoon runs into mid-October. Tea-garden weddings are best between November and early March. Outside that window, you are betting on weather and you need a covered Plan B that the decor team has actually rehearsed.

## Decor that respects the landscape (and the trees)

The single biggest mistake in tea-garden wedding decor is doing too much.

The landscape is finished work. Your job — and ours — is to add a frame, not a painting. Our typical brief:

- A mandap that uses the garden as the backdrop (no full curtain wall behind it)
- Lantern paths from drop-off to ceremony area
- Cane-and-cotton dining furniture, not gold-and-velvet
- Florals in seasonal tones — never the standard "Indian wedding red-and-gold"
- Lighting that goes warm-white at 5 PM and stays there

We have a separate playbook for what we will and will not attach to trees. Estates are increasingly strict on this — never nail, never glue, never rope-tighten around living bark.

## Catering, alcohol permits and the small print

Tea-garden estates rarely have in-house catering at wedding scale. We bring catering up from Siliguri and stage it in a temporary kitchen near the bungalow. Plan for 30% longer service times than a hotel ballroom.

Alcohol permits: West Bengal requires a temporary licence for any event serving alcohol commercially. Most families work around this by hosting privately and not selling drinks — your wedding planner should confirm in writing.

The small print to ask the estate, in writing:

- Power load and generator backup
- Water access for catering
- Permitted setup time (always ask for previous-day setup)
- Noise cutoff time
- Permitted attachments to trees, structures and grass

## Realistic guest counts and what they cost in budget bands

A tea-garden wedding is not a budget choice. The decor pricing band is firmly ₹₹₹ because we are building a temporary venue inside an outdoor footprint — generator, kitchen, drape walls, mandap structure, weather contingency.

Guest count guidance:

- **40–80 guests** — works in nearly any of the estates we have mentioned. Most cinematic format.
- **80–150 guests** — needs careful estate selection and stronger weather Plan B.
- **150–250 guests** — possible at 2–3 Dooars properties only.
- **250+ guests** — book a hotel in Siliguri instead. Trust us.
