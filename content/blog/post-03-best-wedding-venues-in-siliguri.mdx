We do not sell venues — we light them. That means we have stood in nearly every wedding-ready hall and lawn between Sevoke Road and Matigara, and we know which ones have a 12-foot ceiling that lets a chandelier breathe, which ones have an awkward pillar that needs a floral column to hide, and which ones the staff actually let you set up the previous evening.

This is a working list of nine venues we have designed weddings in. Honest notes, not affiliate recommendations.

## How to read a venue brief in 30 seconds

Before the list — the four numbers that matter:

1. **Ceiling height** — under 11 feet kills tall florals and chandeliers.
2. **Pillar layout** — a centrally-located structural pillar costs you about 12% of your decor budget to "hide."
3. **Power load** — under 30 kVA limits your lighting and AV ambitions; ask the venue manager directly.
4. **Setup window** — same-day setup forces decor to be simpler. Previous-evening access lets us do the work that photographs well.

If a venue can't tell you those four numbers in your first walkthrough, you have your answer.

## Banquet halls on Sevoke Road and Hill Cart Road

The independent banquet halls along these corridors are the workhorse venues for Siliguri weddings — typically 150 to 400 guests, hall rate plus catering minimum.

**Halls we've designed in**:

- A Sevoke Road hall with a 14-foot ceiling and a wide central aisle — works beautifully for a [Bengali wedding mandap](/services/bengali-wedding) at the front and round dining behind.
- A Hill Cart Road hall closer to the city centre — lower ceiling (11 feet), but excellent power load and a friendly setup team.

These venues are price-band ₹₹ for decor. They expect a previous-evening setup of 4–6 hours.

## Hotel ballrooms (Mainak, Sinclairs, Cygnett, Royal Sarovar)

Hotel ballrooms in Siliguri sit one step above banquet halls in service polish and one step above in rate.

- **Hotel Mainak** — central, large ballroom, lift access for elderly guests, strict on flame use and stain-risk decor. Excellent for [reception evenings](/services/reception).
- **Sinclairs Siliguri** — slightly out of the city core, dedicated wedding floor, accommodating with setup time, in-house catering well-reviewed by Bengali families.
- **Cygnett Lite Eleven Mile** — newer property, contemporary interior — pairs well with editorial decor briefs that lean into restraint.
- **Royal Sarovar Portico** — large pool-side lawn option for a daytime Mehendi or Haldi if the weather is right.

All hotel ballrooms are price-band ₹₹–₹₹₹ for decor depending on guest count and ceremony scope.

## Outdoor and resort venues around Salugara and Matigara

Outdoor venues are where Siliguri weddings get interesting. The light is better, the photograph background is better, and the rituals breathe.

- A resort property near Salugara with a covered central lawn — the lawn carries 300 standing easily and the covered area handles weather contingency. Excellent for a 2-day Bengali wedding format.
- A Matigara-area venue with a separate Sangeet-night lawn and a covered Bibaho hall — couples who want each function to feel different (rather than three nights in the same room) gravitate here.

Outdoor venues need rain plans built into the decor brief from day one. We always brief two layouts — one optimistic, one wet — and the venue manager signs both off.

## Lawn and open-air spaces for winter weddings

November through February, lawn weddings in Siliguri are a serious option. The risk is morning fog (Dooars-facing venues can be unusable until 10 AM) and evening cold past 9:30 PM.

We design winter lawn weddings with:

- A heated, drape-walled "warming area" behind the seating
- Lantern-led pathway lighting (not floodlights)
- A mandap covered by a tensile roof — not just open canopy
- Heaters at every cluster of 8 chairs

Lawn weddings are typically price-band ₹₹₹ for decor — you are essentially building a temporary indoor space in an outdoor footprint.

## Booking, decor lead time and the questions venue managers won't answer

Six questions to ask every venue, in writing:

1. What is the previous-evening setup window?
2. What is your in-house catering minimum, and can decor staff eat from it?
3. What is the power load and where are the breakers?
4. Are there restrictions on naked flame, smoke machines, or floor adhesive?
5. Will you give us a written guarantee that no other event runs in parallel?
6. What's your cancellation and date-change policy for monsoon disruption?

The honest answer to question six tells you more about the venue than any photograph on their Instagram.
