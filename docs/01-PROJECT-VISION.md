# 01 — Project Vision & Brand

## 1.1 The one-line pitch

> **Siligurievent — North Bengal's most cinematic event decorators. We design celebrations that feel like film stills.**

## 1.2 Brand positioning

| Axis | Position |
|---|---|
| Price | Mid-premium → premium. Not cheap; not unaffordable. |
| Style | Modern editorial luxury with Indian soul. Restrained palette, generous negative space, hero moments. |
| Region | North Bengal authority — Siliguri, Bagdogra, Darjeeling, Jalpaiguri, Kalimpong, Gangtok, Naxalbari. |
| Vibe | Cinematic, calm, confident. The opposite of cluttered marigold-pile websites. |
| Trust signal | Case studies with named clients (with consent), real photography, real numbers, real reviews. |

## 1.3 What we are NOT

- ❌ Not a cheap-and-fast banquet decorator. We're the studio you book 6 months in advance.
- ❌ Not a marketplace or aggregator.
- ❌ Not generic "wedding website template" energy.
- ❌ Not loud or gaudy. Even the most opulent installations are photographed with restraint.

## 1.4 Audience segments

### A. Bride & groom (and their parents) — 60% of leads
- Age 25–35 (couples), 50–65 (parents who often pay & decide).
- Researching on phone, late evenings, comparing 3–5 decorators.
- Want: confidence the team is reliable, visual proof of past weddings, honest pricing bands (so they can self-qualify), easy WhatsApp reach.
- Will share the site to family WhatsApp groups — so social previews must be stunning.

### B. Parents planning life-event ceremonies — 20%
- Annaprashan, Naamkaran, Mukhe Bhaat, Godh Bharai, milestone birthdays, 25th/50th anniversaries.
- Less budget per event but high frequency. Strong word-of-mouth driver.

### C. Corporate / institutional — 10%
- Hotels, schools, hospitals, banks needing annual day, conferences, product launches.
- Want: portfolio + GST invoice + reliability.

### D. Festival pujas — 10%
- Apartment complexes / cultural associations.
- Durga Puja, Lakshmi Puja, Saraswati Puja, Kali Puja.
- Seasonal but lucrative. Bengal-specific moat.

## 1.5 Brand voice

- **Confident, never boastful.** "We work with 30 families a year by design" not "We are the BEST decorators!"
- **Specific, not generic.** Use real venue names, real flower names (rajnigandha, glads, anthurium), real ceremony names (Gaye Holud, Subho Drishti, Bidaai).
- **Warm but not saccharine.** No "magical fairytale" cliches. Think editorial magazine, not Pinterest caption.
- **Bilingual texture.** Occasional Bengali/Hindi proper nouns kept in their script as headings or accents, with English explanation in body copy.

### Voice examples

✅ "Mandap, mirror-finished. Forty thousand jasmines, individually strung the night before."

✅ "From Subho Drishti to Bashor Ghor — we stage every chapter of a Bengali wedding."

❌ "Your dream wedding awaits! ✨💖 Book us NOW for the BEST decorations!"

❌ "Magical fairytale decorations for your special day."

## 1.6 Unique selling propositions (USPs)

1. **North Bengal native.** We understand Bengali, Marwari, Nepali, Hindu, Sikh, Muslim, and Christian ceremony nuances — your guest list won't see a generic template applied to a Bengali ashirbaad or a nikah.
2. **Single decorator end-to-end.** Same lead designer from first call to last candle. No vendor handoffs.
3. **Cinematic photography of every job.** Every event we decorate is shot for our portfolio — and you get the photos.
4. **Honest pricing bands, custom from there.** Every package shows its band (₹ / ₹₹ / ₹₹₹) with a plain-English range of what typically fits in it. No "let's see your budget" sales tactics; no specific INR figures published either (see `DECISIONS.md` D-002).
5. **Tea-garden access.** We have signed venue partners across Darjeeling and Dooars tea estates — open-air sundowner weddings with a backdrop you can't get in Kolkata.

## 1.7 Visual mood

Reference vocabulary (curate Pinterest board around these terms):

- **Photography**: golden hour, soft directional light, shallow depth-of-field, 35mm and 85mm primes feel, film grain, muted highlights, deep shadows.
- **Composition**: rule of thirds broken purposefully, negative space, layered foreground/background, subject off-center.
- **Palette in shots**: warm whites, jasmine cream, terracotta, brass, burgundy velvet, midnight blue silk, single accent (single-stem rose vs a wall of flowers).
- **Avoid**: harsh on-camera flash, oversaturated reds, every-color-in-one-frame, drone shots showing chaos.
